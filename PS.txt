PS 1.3   
Meeting Accountability Gap 
Build a meeting outcome tracker focused purely on decisions and action items 
 
Background 
Teams hold an average of 11 to 15 meetings per week. Action items are discussed, nodded at and 
promptly forgotten. Studies show that within 48 hours, most participants have forgotten 50 to 70 percent 
of what was decided. Meetings repeat the same conversations. Decisions get re-debated. The person 
who was supposed to do something never received a written record of the commitment. Accountability 
evaporates — not because people are irresponsible, but because there is no lightweight system to 
capture it. 
 
The Problem in Detail 
• Meeting notes are either not taken, or taken in a personal notebook no one else can see 
• Action items discussed verbally are rarely assigned a named owner and a concrete deadline 
• Follow-up emails summarising meetings are inconsistently written and quickly buried in inboxes 
• There is no single place where a person can see all the tasks they owe across multiple 
meetings 
• Overdue action items have no visible consequence — they silently age until someone asks 
about them 
 
The Challenge 
Build a focused meeting outcome tracker — not a transcription tool, not a scheduler — that captures 
decisions and action items in real time and keeps every participant accountable to their commitments. 
 
What to Build 
Your submission must address the following functional areas. A complete, well-executed subset is 
better than a broken full implementation. 
 
1 Meeting log — user creates a meeting record with title, date, a list of attendees and a brief 
agenda or context note 
2 Decisions log — user adds key decisions made during the meeting (what was decided, not 
who said what); decisions are displayed prominently 
3 Action items — user adds action items with a title, an assigned owner selected from attendees, 
a deadline date and a status: open, in progress or done 
4 Personal feed — each attendee has a view showing only their action items across all 
meetings, sorted by deadline, with overdue items highlighted 
5 Staleness indicator — any action item past its deadline and not marked done is flagged 
visually and surfaced prominently in both the meeting view and the personal feed 
6 Weekly digest — a summary view showing all decisions made this week, all action items due 
this week, overdue items and items completed this week
